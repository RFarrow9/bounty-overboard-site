BOUNTY OVERBOARD

Captain Wilf Marlow is a day off retiring when the Cosmic Bank freezes his pension and hides it across eleven dimensions. He's fairly annoyed about it. He's getting it back with cannons.

A top-down pirate game. If you loved Overboard! on the PS1, or TimeSplitters, or Banjo-Kazooie, you'll feel at home.

The job

One last job. For his pension.

Wilf has done forty-one years under the black flag. The last two were behind a desk as a "pirate-risk consultant", which he doesn't like to talk about. Then the bank froze his pension and stashed it across eleven dimensional branch offices, one signed token apiece, each guarded by a regional manager with targets to hit and no intention of countersigning anything.

So he's sailing through all eleven to collect. The final boss is HR. Obviously it is.

It's a spiritual successor to Overboard! / Shipwreckers! (PS1, 1997), built in Godot 4.6, and it's a bit daft on purpose.

The arsenal

Loads of toys. The fun is stacking them.

Every weapon leaves a status on whatever it hits. Statuses stack, and that's where it gets silly. Line the right two up and you get something much nastier than either on its own.

A few of the combos

Oiled + Fire = Inferno

Drench a fleet in oil, then swan past with the flamethrower. Burns twice as hard and jumps hull to hull.

Frozen + Ram = Shatter

Freeze someone solid, then ram them. That's an instant kill, no follow-up needed.

Oil slick + Tesla arc = free ignition

Park near an oiled ship with the coil live. The arc lights the oil for you. No fire weapon required.

Oil + booster = burning wake

Gun the rocket booster through a slick and you leave a trail of fire behind the boat. Good for a chase.

Rimefang, twice = guaranteed shatter

First spear freezes. Second spear shatters. A clean two-tap finisher.

Electrified = it spreads

Shock one ship and the charge arcs to whoever's unlucky enough to be sailing next to it.
