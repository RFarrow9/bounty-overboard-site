The job

A cosmic heist, one form at a time

A top-down pirate action game — a spiritual successor to Overboard! / Shipwreckers! (PS1, 1997), built in Godot 4.6.

Captain Wilf Marlow: forty-one years under the black flag, the last two stuck behind a desk as a "pirate-risk consultant." He's one day from retirement — and he knows it's a cursed thing to say in an action story. Everyone does. Nobody lets him live it down.

Eleven dimensional branch offices. Eleven surly regional managers with unreasonable KPIs. Eleven signed tokens to prise loose before the final boss — HR — countersigns the paperwork.

Aboard the sloop

A ship, an arsenal, and a grudge

The arsenal

Staggered broadsides, chain shot, and a forward cannon — plus dingy-industrial, Tesla-style sidearms you bolt on as you go.

  • Flamethrower — cone of fire, ignites oil slicks
  • Mines & Fire Barrels — leave death in your wake
  • Tesla Coil — arc that chains between enemies
  • Rimefang — freeze on the first hit, shatter on the second
  • Kraken's Blessing — summon a god, briefly

…plus eleven dimension-locked artifacts. Take them off the manager.

The cast

  • Captain Wilf — gruff, genre-savvy, entirely self-interested. Just wants to hang up the flag.
  • Beakwell — a snarky seagull who does the emotional labour.
  • The Dimensionalist — CEO of the Cosmic Bank. Speaks only in corporate euphemism. "Let's loop back on that."
  • HR — the final boss. Of course it is.
